## Why These Guidelines Exist

SafeChat was built on a simple premise: **everyone deserves to participate online without being attacked, harassed, or demeaned.** These guidelines describe what kind of community we are building together.

Our AI moderation system blocks harmful content automatically, but the spirit of these rules matters more than the letter. When in doubt, ask yourself: *would I be comfortable if this were said to someone I cared about?*

---

## What's Welcome on SafeChat

- Genuine self-expression and creativity
- Authentic conversations with friends, family, and new connections
- Sharing what matters to you — your interests, work, life moments
- Respectful disagreement and dialogue across different perspectives
- Constructive feedback and support
- Communities that celebrate identity, culture, and shared interests
- Humor that doesn't come at someone's expense

---

## What's Not Allowed

### 1. Bullying and Harassment

We have zero tolerance for content that targets individuals to harm them. This includes:

- Personal attacks, insults, or name-calling
- Mocking someone's appearance, abilities, identity, or circumstances
- Repeated unwanted contact after someone has indicated they want it to stop
- Coordinated harassment (multiple people targeting one person)
- Sharing embarrassing photos or information to humiliate
- Encouraging self-harm or suicide
- Mocking someone's grief, trauma, or hardship

### 2. Hate Speech

Content that attacks people based on protected characteristics is not allowed:

- Race, ethnicity, national origin
- Religion or religious practices
- Caste
- Gender, gender identity, gender expression
- Sexual orientation
- Disability or medical conditions
- Age

This includes slurs, dehumanizing language, calls for discrimination, and content that promotes hatred — even if framed as humor.

### 3. Threats and Violence

Direct or indirect threats are not allowed:

- Threats of physical harm, sexual violence, or death
- Wishing harm on someone
- Statements that could reasonably be interpreted as intimidation
- Glorifying past violence against individuals
- Content that incites others to commit violence

### 4. Sexual Content and Exploitation

SafeChat is not a platform for sexual content:

- Sexually explicit images, videos, or text
- Solicitation of sexual content or services
- Content that sexualizes minors — this results in immediate ban and reporting to authorities
- Non-consensual intimate imagery (revenge porn)
- Unwanted sexual advances in messages

### 5. Privacy Violations

Don't share information that isn't yours to share:

- Personal details (address, phone number, workplace) of others without consent
- Private messages or screenshots shared to embarrass someone
- Photos or videos of people taken without their knowledge or consent
- Information that could identify minors against their parents' wishes
- Doxxing — gathering and publishing private information to enable harassment

### 6. Impersonation

Don't pretend to be someone you're not:

- Creating accounts using someone else's name, photos, or identity
- Operating fan accounts without clearly identifying them as such
- Posing as an official organization, government, or business you don't represent

Parody and satire are acceptable when clearly labeled and not designed to deceive.

### 7. Spam and Manipulation

We protect against attempts to abuse the platform:

- Mass posting of identical content
- Buying or selling accounts, followers, or engagement
- Phishing attempts, scams, or fraudulent schemes
- Coordinated inauthentic behavior (bot networks)
- Vote or rating manipulation
- Misleading clickbait

### 8. Illegal Content

Content that violates the law is removed:

- Promotion or sale of illegal goods (drugs, weapons, stolen items)
- Content that infringes copyright or trademarks
- Promotion of human trafficking or exploitation
- Content that violates sanctions or export controls

### 9. Misinformation That Causes Harm

We focus on misinformation that has potential to cause real-world harm:

- Health misinformation that could lead to dangerous behavior
- Election misinformation designed to suppress voting
- Manipulated media presented as authentic
- Coordinated disinformation campaigns

We do not arbitrate political opinions, religious beliefs, or matters of legitimate debate.

### 10. Glorifying Harm

Content that celebrates or promotes harm:

- Self-harm methods or pro-suicide content
- Pro-eating disorder content
- Substance abuse glorification
- Animal cruelty
- Terrorism or extremist violence

## How Enforcement Works

### Automatic blocking

Our AI moderation system blocks content before it reaches recipients. When this happens, the sender is notified that their content didn't meet our guidelines. Specific reasons are not disclosed to prevent users from learning to bypass the system.

### Human review

Some content is held for human review:
- Posts and stories flagged as potentially harmful
- Content reported by multiple users
- Appeals from users whose content was blocked

### Account actions

Depending on severity and frequency of violations, we may:

| Action | When |
|---|---|
| Content removal | First-time minor violations |
| Warning | Repeated minor violations |
| Temporary feature restrictions | Patterns of borderline behavior |
| Temporary account suspension | Serious violations |
| Permanent ban | Severe violations or repeated offenses |
| Report to authorities | Illegal content, threats, child exploitation |

### Severe violations result in immediate action

The following lead to immediate account termination without warning:
- Content sexualizing minors
- Credible threats of violence
- Coordinated harassment campaigns
- Hate speech with calls to action
- Distribution of intimate imagery without consent

## How to Report Violations

If you see content that violates these guidelines:

1. Tap the **Report** button on the content
2. Select the reason that best matches
3. Optionally add details
4. Submit

We review reports within 24 hours for severe violations and within 7 days for other reports.

For emergencies (immediate danger to yourself or others), contact local emergency services first, then report to us.

---

## Appealing Decisions

If you believe your content was incorrectly blocked or your account incorrectly restricted:

1. Open the in-app **Help** section
2. Select **Appeal a decision**
3. Provide details about why you believe the decision was incorrect
4. Submit

Human moderators review appeals. We respond within 7 business days. If your appeal is successful, the restriction is reversed.

---

## Protecting Yourself

We provide tools to control your experience:

- **Block** any user — they can no longer interact with you
- **Mute** any user — you don't see their content, but they can still see yours
- **Restrict messages** to followers only or no one
- **Make your account private** — only approved followers see your content
- **Hide comments** containing specific keywords
- **Report** any content or account

If you are experiencing harassment, please use these tools and report violations. We are here to help.

---

## Special Protections

### Minors (under 18)

Accounts identified as belonging to minors receive additional protections:
- Private account by default
- Messaging restricted to mutual followers
- Content moderation thresholds are stricter
- Suggested friends limited to age peers

If you suspect an account is operated by someone under the minimum age, report it.

### Public figures and journalists

Public figures may receive more critical commentary about their public actions and statements. However, our rules against harassment, hate speech, and threats still apply to content about anyone.

## How These Guidelines Evolve

We update these guidelines as:
- New patterns of abuse emerge
- User feedback identifies gaps
- Legal requirements change
- We learn from moderation outcomes

Material changes are announced via in-app notification. Continued use of SafeChat after updates indicates acceptance.

We built SafeChat because the internet should be a place where people can connect, share, and grow — not where they go to be attacked. We know we won't get every moderation decision right. We know our AI will sometimes block things it shouldn't, and sometimes miss things it should catch.

What we promise is this: we will keep working to improve. We will listen when users tell us we got something wrong.
